The Paul Problem

Paul (Saul of Tarsus) never met Jesus in his lifetime, yet his writings form the theological foundation of Christianity. He claimed authority through a vision on the road to Damascus.

Paul taught that the Law was a curse (Galatians 3:13), while Jesus said he came not to abolish the Law but to fulfill it (Matthew 5:17).

Paul claimed apostleship by his own testimony, while the original disciples were chosen by Jesus himself.

"I have been crucified with Christ. It is no longer I who live, but Christ who lives in me." — Paul's theology centers on crucifixion, yet Jesus in the Gospels emphasizes obedience and submission to God's will.

The Quran confirms Jesus was not crucified: "They did not kill him, nor did they crucify him, but [another] was made to resemble him to them." (4:157)
